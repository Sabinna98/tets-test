THIS IS MY TEST-2!!!!Letter of J.D. Salinger, New York, May 29, 1957

First exercise

    1.

    Dear (Mr.) MisterKanin,

    I'm afraid what you've heard is very true - I don't see any of my fictional characters in the theater - but I'm glad you liked ZOOEY,and glad you thought and bothered to write. My stand about the theater isn't a very happy one, considering that there are so many fine people putting on plays - I'm thinking especially of Elia Kazan, whom I like very much - but I have an old and unshakable notion that I know what's best for my characters.

    Thank you, though.
    Sincerely,
    J.D. Salinger

      TO MY MOTHER
      1

      If you really want to hear about it, the first thing you'll probably want to know is where I was born, and what my lousy childhood was like...

      Where I want to start telling is the day I left Pencey Prep. Pencey Prep is this school that's in Agerstown, Pennsylvania...

      Anyway, it was the Saturday of the football game with Saxon Hall...

      There were never many girls at all at the football games...

      The reason I was standing way up on Thomsen Hill...

      The other reason I wasn't down at the game was because I was on my way to say good-by to old Spencer...

      I forgot to tell you about that. They kicked me out...

      Anyway, it was December and all, and it was cold as a witch's teat...

      I was lucky. All of a sudden I thought of something that helped make me know I was getting the hell out...

      I ran all the way to the main gate, and then I waited a second till I got my breath...

        If you really want to hear about it, the first thing you'll probably want to know is where I was born, and what my lousy childhood was like, and how my parents were occupied and all before they had me, and all that David Copperfield kind of crap, but I don't feel like going into it, if you want to know the truth. In the first place, that stuff bores me, and in the second place, my parents would have about two hemorrhages apiece if I told anything pretty personal about them. They're quite touchy about anything like that, especially my father. They're nice and all--I'm not saying that--but they're also touchy as hell. Besides, I'm not going to tell you my whole goddam autobiography or anything. I'll just tell you about this madman stuff that happened to me around last Christmas just before I got pretty run-down and had to come out here and take it easy. I mean that's all I told D.B. about, and he's my brother and all. He's in Hollywood. That isn't too far from this crumby place, and he comes over and visits me practically every weekend. He's going to drive me home when I go home next month maybe.
